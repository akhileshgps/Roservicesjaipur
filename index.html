💧Why is RO Essential in Jaipur?

⚠️Common RO Problems & Solutions

These are the most common RO water purifier problems reported by Jaipur customers. Recognize the signs early and get them fixed before they get worse:

💧

Low Water Output from RO

The sediment filter is clogged or the RO membrane is damaged. Ignoring this puts extra load on the pump — get the filter changed or membrane replaced immediately.

🔢

High TDS in Output Water

This is a sign of a damaged RO membrane. If output TDS exceeds 150 mg/L, get the membrane replaced immediately — a faulty membrane also fails to filter bacteria.

🔊

Unusual Noise from RO

The pump is wearing out or there is air trapped in the pipes. Don't ignore this — the pump can fail completely. Get it checked by a technician.

🚿

Water Leaking from RO

Pipe fittings are loose or the O-ring is damaged. A leak can damage the pump and circuit board — call for service immediately.

🔴

RO Not Turning On

The SMPS power supply or circuit board has failed. Do not attempt to fix this yourself — always call a professional technician.

🤢

Bad Taste or Smell in Water

The post-carbon filter has degraded or bacteria has grown in the storage tank. Get tank sanitization and post-carbon filter replacement done immediately.

🔄RO Filter Change Guide — When & How?

Your RO's performance depends entirely on the condition of its filters. Changing them on time is critical — otherwise water quality suffers and the pump gets overloaded.

🧹

Sediment Filter

Replace every 3–6 months. Filters dirt, sand and rust from water.

⚫

Pre Carbon Filter

Replace every 6 months. Removes chlorine and eliminates bad odour.

💎

RO Membrane

Replace every 1–2 years. Filters TDS, bacteria and heavy metals.

✨

Post Carbon Filter

Replace every 6–12 months. Improves the final taste of water.

Due to Jaipur's high TDS levels, filters tend to clog faster than the standard timeline. It's advisable to check them 1–2 months ahead of schedule. With an AMC package, all of this is tracked and managed automatically.

Check output TDS regularly with a TDS meter — above 150 mg/L means the membrane needs replacement
Run the RO for at least 2–3 hours daily — keeping it off for long periods allows bacteria to grow
Get the storage tank sanitized at least once a year
Always use original branded filters — duplicate filters damage the membrane faster
Keep at least 2 feet of clear space around the RO unit — proper air circulation is essential

How often should RO filters be changed?▼
The sediment filter should be changed every 3–6 months, the pre-carbon filter every 6 months, the RO membrane every 1–2 years, and the post-carbon filter every 6–12 months. Due to Jaipur's high TDS, filters tend to clog faster. If water flow reduces or the taste changes, get your filters checked immediately.

Why is my RO producing less water than usual?▼
The most common reasons for low RO output are: a clogged sediment filter, a damaged RO membrane, low inlet water pressure (below 10 PSI), a faulty booster pump, or a blocked drain line. In Jaipur's high-TDS areas, the sediment filter clogs up especially fast. Get a professional technician to diagnose the issue accurately.
